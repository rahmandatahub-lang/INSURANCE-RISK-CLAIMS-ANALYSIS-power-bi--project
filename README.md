Insurance companies manage large volumes of policyholder and claims data across multiple systems. Due to scattered data sources, stakeholders often face challenges in tracking performance, identifying risk patterns, and making timely data-driven decisions.

This project focuses on building a centralized, interactive Power BI dashboard that provides clear insights into insurance policies, claims behavior, and risk factors using visual analytics.

****Business Objective****
The primary objective of this project is to help the insurance company:
Understand its policyholder base
Analyze claim patterns and financial impact
Identify high-risk customer segments
Support data-driven decision-making for underwriting, pricing, and risk management

**Business Problem**
Policy and claims data are spread across multiple sources
No single dashboard to track KPIs and trends
Difficulty in identifying risk drivers such as age, car type, usage, and demographics
Limited visibility into claim frequency and severity

KPI Requirements
The dashboard highlights the following key performance indicators:
Total Policies
Measures the size of the active policyholder base.
Total Claim Amount
Tracks the overall financial impact of claims.
Claim Frequency
Analyzes how frequently claims are being filed.
Average Claim Amount
Evaluates claim severity and potential risk exposure.
Gender-wise Total Policies
Understands customer distribution across genders for better segmentation.

**Visualization & Analysis Requirements****
To go beyond KPIs, multiple visualizations are used to explore policy and claim data across demographics, vehicle attributes, and customer behavior.
